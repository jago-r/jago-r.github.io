Jago Rowe

I am Jago (/d͡ʒeɪgoʊ/ or /d͡ʒagɔ/, or any other pronounciation you can think of), a final year Undergraduate Linguistics Student at University of Cambridge. I am interested in natural and constructed languages from a variety of perspectives, primarily relating to to morphosyntax and typology, but also with an interest in phonetics, historical linguistics and computational linguistics.

My research so far has primarily been concerned with cognition and how this shapes language categories and the orders which they can appear in. I have worked on the Final-over-Final condition, an attested linearisation restriction which bans a head-final phrase from dominating a head-initial one under certain (poorly defined) conditions. I am particularly interested in the prosodic correlations of this, and how we might be able to model this computationally. In my work, I take inspiration from computer science and mathematics, while mixing this with linguistic coverage.

I mainly work with the Indo-European language family, although for work in language prosody I work with Chinese varieties. I am enthusiastic about the Cornish language (that's where my name comes from!), and I am involved with the revived Late Cornish community. I am also passionate about approaching linguistic study from a multimodal perspective, particularly paying attention to signed languages. I am committed to helping coordination between Undergraduate Linguists in the UK and abroad.
